In Other Words

(Linguistics Puzzle)

Colourless green ideas sleep furiously. What makes symbolism, syntax, and sound come together to form a language?

  • Flex your logic skills and linguistic intuition with “In Other Words” - Microsoft and Pravega’s ongoing linguistic puzzle solving contest.
  • Solve and submit levels from Friday 9:00 am to Sunday 12 noon to climb up the leaderboard!
  • Use of all electronic sources and books is allowed.
  • Plagiarism and collusion outside of the team is not permitted.
  • Spot Registration only: Visit the IOW desks at OPB, Main gate and JN Tata Auditorium to get registered. Registrations stay open from 9:00 am, 19th January through 12 noon, 21st January.
  • Exciting prizes await!

Check the leaderboard here.

Instructions

  1. Visit the registration desks at Main Gate, OPB or JN Tata Auditorium to get your name onto the leader-board. The JN Tata desk opens only from 9:00 am on Saturday.
  2. Submit your team’s correspondence email ID and receive the question for level 1 via email.
  3. Scan/Type and submit your solutions to us at our email.
    1. Teams scoring more than the threshold score for a particular level will receive the next level’s question via email.
    2. The score hence achieved will get added to their net score.
    3. Solutions will be revealed after the culmination of the fest.
  5. Solutions will be accepted from 9:00 am to 10:00 pm on Friday, 8:00 am to 6:00 pm on Saturday and 8:00 am to 1:30 pm on Sunday.
  6. The leader-board will update periodically to let teams monitor their progress.

Paper Marbling

(Artistic Workshop)

Paper Marbling is an ancient Japanese artform which involves capturing impressions of ink and color by the floating ink technique. It is a method of aqueous surface design, which can produce patterns similar to smooth marble or other kinds of stone.

If you’ve ever wanted to try an expressive art form with a mind of its own, paper marbling, or Suminagashi (墨 流 し), as it was called by the ancient Japanese when they invented it in the 12th Century, might just be the artform for you. The vibrant and colorful artforms represent every personality; Spontaneous, abstract, random, detail-oriented, improvisational or super-patient.

Everybody is welcome to come and try out this super-fun workshop, where every participant will be taught the technique and then given two attempts to marble a sheet, which they can then take home!

Don’t miss out on this spectacular opportunity to learn something new for absolutely free!

Date 20th & 21st January 2018 (Saturday & Sunday)
Time 10AM to 5PM
Venue Fun Zone
Facilitators Priya Choudhary & Kiran Johar
